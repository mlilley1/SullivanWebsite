the writing community

Stories are most delicious when shared - at bedtime, on the playground, at work, or curled up in a quiet corner with hot cocoa. The process of telling stories is no different. It takes a village!

I could never do what I do as an artist without the patience, feedback, kind encouragement, and lively discussions with fellow artists. When I first dreamt of being a writer, I had no idea that dream would lead me to one of the kindest and most engaged communities around.

I have been a part of two wonderful writing communities, one in Texas and one in Minnesota. Here are some of the great community programs in Texas, Minnesota and beyond.

Welcome to our village. Enjoy conversations about the world of children's books and making art. Find a place to connect with other writers. Be inspired to build community where you are. Most of all, welcome. I'm glad you're here.

Texas

  • The Picture Book Salon of San Antonio.
    Inspired by Molly Beth Griffin's PB Salon in Minnesota, we meet quarterly to discuss all things picture book. Free to attend, all are welcome, and sometimes, we even have donuts!

  • The Southwest Texas branch of the Society of Children's Book Writers and Illustrators (SW TX SCBWI).
    SCBWI has great opportunities for people to get involved and meet other artists.
Someone brought donuts and cookies!

Minnesota

  • Picture Book Salon of Minnesota.
    Hosted by Molly Beth Griffin. They meet once a month to discuss all things picture book. Molly's PB Salon inspired me to start one in Texas!

  • The Minnesota branch of the Society of Children's Book Writers and Illustrators (MN SCBWI).
    SCBWI has great opportunities for people to get involved and meet other artists.

  • The Loft Literary Center.
    The Loft has great classes on children's literature and other kinds of writing.

  • Write On! Radio.
    A program on KFAI Radio, where they talk writing with writers.

and beyond

  • The Society of Children's Book Writers and Illustrators (SCBWI).
    SCBWI has great resources for writers and illustrators, including information on meetups
    and critique groups around the country.

  • The Loft Online Classes.
    In addition to classes in Minnesota, the Loft Literary Center offers online writing classes.

  • Highlights Foundation.
    The Highlights Foundation offers children's literature classes for writers and illustrators.
